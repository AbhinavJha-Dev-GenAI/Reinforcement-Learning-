A collection of seminal and state-of-the-art papers that shaped the field of RL.

## Foundational
1.  **Sutton & Barto (1988)**: *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction*. The "Bible" of RL.
2.  **Watkins & Dayan (1992)**: *Q-Learning*. The formal introduction of the Q-Learning algorithm.

## Deep Reinforcement Learning
3.  **Mnih et al. (2013/2015)**: *Playing Atari with Deep Reinforcement Learning*. The first successful application of deep learning to RL. [Read Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1312.5602)
4.  **Silver et al. (2016)**: *Mastering the game of Go with deep neural networks and tree search (AlphaGo)*. [Read Paper](https://www.nature.com/articles/nature16961)
5.  **Hessel et al. (2017)**: *Rainbow: Combining Improvements in Deep Reinforcement Learning*. [Read Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.02298)

## Policy Gradients & SOTA
6.  **Schulman et al. (2017)**: *Proximal Policy Optimization Algorithms (PPO)*. The standard for robust policy optimization. [Read Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1707.06347)
7.  **Ha & Schmidhuber (2018)**: *World Models*. Introducing the concept of training an agent in its own "dream." [Read Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.10122)
8.  **Ouyang et al. (2022)**: *Training language models to follow instructions with human feedback (InstructGPT)*. The foundation of RLHF. [Read Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.02155)

---

> [!TIP]
> If you are new to RL research, start with the **DQN Atari paper (2013)** and the **PPO paper (2017)**. They are well-written and essential for understanding modern implementations.
